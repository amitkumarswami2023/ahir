General Nursing & Midwifery

Start Your Journey in Nursing, Grounded in Care and Skill

The 3-year GNM program at Ahir College of Nursing equips students with essential nursing competencies, midwifery training, and patient care experience across community and hospital settings. With a focus on hands-on learning, this program prepares students for meaningful careers as bedside caregivers, community health workers, and frontline nursing professionals in both public and private healthcare sectors.

Check out our year-wise learning outcomes:

Key Learnings Semester
Learn the fundamentals of nursing, anatomy & physiology, first aid, and community health. Build essential patient care and communication skills. Semester 1
Study microbiology, psychology, and sociology. Develop basic midwifery and child health skills through early clinical exposure. Semester 2
Key Learnings Semester
Advance into medical-surgical nursing, pharmacology, and nutrition. Train in wards and develop skills in adult and elderly care. Semester 3
Explore mental health nursing and pediatric care. Gain hands-on experience in psychiatric and pediatric departments. Semester 4
Key Learnings Semester
Focus on midwifery, obstetric nursing, and community health. Participate in maternal and newborn care practices. Semester 5
Undergo supervised internship rotations in hospitals and health centers. Prepare for real-world practice and patient responsibility. Semester 6
